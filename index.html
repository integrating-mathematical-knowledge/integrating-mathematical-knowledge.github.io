Motivation
posted on 2022-06-12 23:06:25

I realized that mathematics does not speak exclusively about our real world and instead it speaks about a universe, not our one.

As said in the book "Truth Through Proof A Formalist Foundation for Mathematics" by Alan:
The platonistic conception of mathematics as providing knowledge of the immutable also has, for some, a quasi-religious attraction.
Betrand Russel wrote of the consolation mathematics seemed to offer him, when beset with the woes of our ephemeral and careworn world. Mathematics, wrote Russel, is [r]emote from human passions, remote even from the pitful facts of nature and a realm where pure thought can dwell as in its nature home, where one, at least, of our nobler impulses can scape from the dreary exile of the actual world.
"...the greatest thing is purely mathematical thinking: this is worth much more than the application of mathematics."
In conversation in 1854, a few months before his death, that was. In Gauss, Titan of Science by G. Waldo Dunnington, p. 303.
